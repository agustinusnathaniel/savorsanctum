# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Food enthusiasts and curious explorers looking for curated culinary recommendations. They browse casually, discovering new food places, products, and reading material. Context: relaxed browsing, often on mobile, looking for inspiration rather than utility.

## Product Purpose

SavorSanctum is a curated directory of culinary experiences: food places, products, and reading materials. It leverages Notion as a CMS to keep content fresh without code changes. Success looks like users discovering something new and feeling inspired to explore further.

## Brand Personality

**Warm, playful, inviting.** The interface should feel like a trusted friend sharing their favorite finds, not a corporate review site. Fredoka font and rounded shapes reinforce this. Voice is casual but curated, enthusiastic but not loud.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: no corporate templates, boring card grids, or blue-gray palettes
- **Yelp/Zomato**: no cluttered review-site layouts, star-rating spam, or ad-driven noise
- **Pinterest**: no infinite masonry chaos, visual overload, or pin-board aesthetics

## Design Principles

1. **Curation over volume**: Every item feels hand-picked. White space and restraint signal quality.
2. **Warmth without clutter**: The palette and typography feel inviting, but the layout stays clean and navigable.
3. **Discovery as delight**: Browsing should feel exploratory and fun, not like searching a database.
4. **Show, don't tell**: Let food imagery and concise copy do the persuading, not feature lists or marketing speak.
5. **Practice what you preach**: A food site should look appetizing. The design itself should make you hungry.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance target
- Respect reduced-motion preferences for animations
- Ensure sufficient contrast for all text on colored backgrounds
- Keyboard navigable throughout
